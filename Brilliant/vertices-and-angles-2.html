Points A,B,C,DA,B,C,DA,B,C,D and FFF all lie on a circle with center EEE. As ∠FAB\angle FAB∠FAB and ∠FEB\angle FEB∠FEB are subtended by the same chord FBFBFB we then know from our knowledge of inscribed angles that

∠FAB=12∠FEB=12×60∘=30∘\angle FAB = \dfrac{1}{2} \angle FEB = \dfrac{1}{2} \times 60^{\circ} = \boxed{30^{\circ}}∠FAB=21​∠FEB=21​×60∘=30∘​.

Comment: Let MMM be the point of intersection of ABABAB and EFEFEF. As ∠EBM=45∘\angle EBM = 45^{\circ}∠EBM=45∘ we have that ∠AMF=∠EMB=75∘\angle AMF = \angle EMB = 75^{\circ}∠AMF=∠EMB=75∘, and so as ∠FAB=30∘\angle FAB = 30^{\circ}∠FAB=30∘ we have that ∠AMF=75∘\angle AMF = 75^{\circ}∠AMF=75∘ as well. Thus ΔAMF\Delta AMFΔAMF is isosceles, which I thought was an outcome worth noting.

As we should know, any square can be inscribed inside a circle, in this case, of center in EEE. Here EF‾≅ED‾\overline{EF} \cong \overline{ED}EF≅ED are radii of the circle and, therefore ∠BAF\angle BAF∠BAF is inscribed in the circle, enclosing the same arc as ∠BEF\angle BEF∠BEF, then: ∠BEF=2⋅∠BAF⇒∠BAF=30∘\angle BEF = 2\cdot \angle BAF \Rightarrow \angle BAF = 30^{\circ}∠BEF=2⋅∠BAF⇒∠BAF=30∘
