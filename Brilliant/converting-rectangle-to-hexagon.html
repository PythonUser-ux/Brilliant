Converting rectangle to hexagon

Geometry Level 4

True, False, or Conditionally True?

We can cut any rectangle into three pieces using straight lines, and rearrange these pieces into a convex hexagon whose sides are all of the same length.

True Conditionally true - Only works for squares False

To construct the solution, we proceed as shown below.

Make EEE is a midpoint of the shorter side (or midpoint of any side if ABCDABCDABCD is a square). Find midpoint FFF of AEAEAE. Draw a line perpendicular to AEAEAE through FFF. GGG is an intersection of this line with ABABAB. This construction guarantees that AG=GEAG=GEAG=GE. Cut along GEGEGE, and symmetrically below. Move pieces as shown above.

The construction will fail, that is the line through FFF will not intersect the segment ABABAB, only if BC>2ABBC>2ABBC>2AB or it is equal to it. Since we picked the smaller of the two sides (or one of the sides of a square), this cannot happen.
