7 little circles

Geometry Level 3

7 non-overlapping unit circles are inscribed inside a large circle as shown below. What is the area of the shaded region?

Round your answer to 3 decimal places.


The answer is 0.886.

Relevant wiki: Length and Area - Composite Figures

For this problem, we set the following:

  • Points AAA and BBB are points on the circumference of both circles with centers A′A'A′ and BBB.
  • Point OOO is the center of the largest center, which is also the center of the central circle.

Since all smallest circles have radius of 111, then ∣AO∣=∣AA′∣+∣A′O∣=1+2=3|AO| = |AA'| + |A'O| = 1 + 2 = 3∣AO∣=∣AA′∣+∣A′O∣=1+2=3, which shows that the radius of the largest circle is 333. Likewise, due to symmetry, ∣BO∣=3|BO| = 3∣BO∣=3. Because these mutually tangent circles have same radii, ∠O=60∘\angle O = 60^{\circ}∠O=60∘. In this case, the area of the large circular sector is AL=π6⋅(3)2=3π2A_{L} = \dfrac{\pi}{6} \cdot (3)^2 = \dfrac{3\pi}{2}AL​=6π​⋅(3)2=23π​.

Since we are interested in determining the area of the shaded region, the next step is to determine the area of the white region within the dashed region. Here are two areas we need to determine: (1) area of two sectors and (2) dashed equilateral triangle.

Equilateral Triangle: Since the segments between their centers form the equilateral triangle of side length 222, the area of the indicated equilateral triangle is AΔ=34⋅22=3A_{\Delta} = \dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4} \cdot 2^2 = \sqrt{3}AΔ​=43​​⋅22=3​.

Sector: Angle chasing, we see that ∠AA′B′=∠BB′A′=120∘\angle AA'B' = \angle BB'A' = 120^{\circ}∠AA′B′=∠BB′A′=120∘. Since we are then interested in determining the area of the two sectors that enclose the shaded region, the remaining area to be removed is Ac=π3A_c = \dfrac{\pi}{3}Ac​=3π​.

Therefore, the area of the shaded region is Ashaded=AL−(AΔ+2Ac)=3π2−(3+2π3)=5π6−3≈0.886 \begin{array}{rl} A_{\text{shaded}} &= A_L - \left(A_{\Delta} + 2A_c\right)\\ &= \dfrac{3\pi}{2} - \left(\sqrt{3} + \dfrac{2\pi}{3}\right)\\ &= \dfrac{5\pi}{6} - \sqrt{3} \approx \boxed{0.886} \end{array} Ashaded​​=AL​−(AΔ​+2Ac​)=23π​−(3​+32π​)=65π​−3​≈0.886​​
