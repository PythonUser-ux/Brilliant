Number Theory Level 1

For what integer nnn, is n!n!n! equal to the number of seconds in 6 weeks?

Try to solve this without using a calculator.


The answer is 10.

There are 60 seconds in a minute, 60 minutes in an hour, 24 hours in a day, 7 days in a week. Hence n!=60×60×24×7×6n!=60\times 60\times 24\times 7\times 6n!=60×60×24×7×6. We know that n!n!n! contains 222 factors of 555, hence n≥10n\geq 10n≥10. However, n!n!n! does not contain a factor of 111111, hence n<11n<11n<11. Conclusion: n=10n=\boxed{10}n=10​.

For those who know right off the bat that 242424 is equal to 4!4!4!, it becomes apparent that we are almost finished calculating up to an nnn of 777 already since there are 242424 hours in a day, 777 days in a week, and 666 weeks to solve for:

7!=4!×7×6×57! = 4! \times 7 \times 6 \times 57!=4!×7×6×5.

We have everything except a 555 to complete our calculation up to 7!7!7! The only thing left to do, then, is to isolate a factor of 5 from what's left to solve. What's left is the number of seconds in an hour, which we can quickly determine to be 360036003600.

Thus, n!=7×6×5×4!×36005→7!×720→7!×(8×9×10)→10!n! = 7 \times 6 \times 5 \times 4! \times \frac{3600}{5} \rightarrow 7! \times 720 \rightarrow 7! \times (8 \times 9 \times 10) \rightarrow 10! n!=7×6×5×4!×53600​→7!×720→7!×(8×9×10)→10!

n=10\boxed{n = 10} n=10​
